A **DWY (Done-With-You) offer** is a service model where you and the client **work together** to build or execute something, instead of you doing it all for them (DFY: Done-For-You) or just teaching them (DIY: Do-It-Yourself).

In this scenario, the **client is paying for Scripe (instead of you)** and you both collaborate on their workspace.

Here's how to collaborate in Scripe on a DWY offer:

### 3. Meet in your Scripe Introduction Call

Complete the Scripe Setup with your client and introduce them to all relevant workflows.

**Checklist for your Scripe Intro Calls with Clients:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Ensure onboarding is complete">
    Ensure the client has completed the set up list you sent them and most importantly, that the Chrome Extension is installed.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Explain how Scripe works best">
    Scripe works best when it truly understands you. The more context Scripe has about your business, goals, and voice, the better the content, ideas, and recommendations it generates.

    There are two levels of personalization in Scripe:

    - **Personal Brand:** this level is essential. If you‚Äôre working with a single brand or profile, it‚Äôs the only configuration you need for Scripe to deliver accurate, on-brand content.
    - **Workspace:** only necessary if you manage more than one Personal Brand in the same workspace (for example, a company with multiple employees that need them to follow a specific tone of voice).

    The steps for completing personalization are outlined below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Upload min. 2 files to the Knowledge Base">
    The Knowledge Base is Scripe‚Äôs **long-term memory**.

    It‚Äôs where you store everything Scripe should always know about you, your business, and your expertise ‚Äî so you don‚Äôt have to explain it again every time you create content.

    The Knowledge Base works best when it includes relevant materials, such as:

    - Sales decks or one-pagers
    - Case studies or success stories
    - Website copy, landing pages, or blog posts
    - ICP or persona descriptions
    - Recorded calls, webinars, or podcasts

    <Tip>
      Want to give even more value to your client? Use our ICP Questionnaire template to define their ICP, fill it out together, and then upload it to the Knowledge Base.

      **Get your template in:**

      - [German](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1_INzccuwM-ebcP2KZmEBJ1NMomHSYqCIpaqKzhyPReQ/edit?usp=sharing)
      - [English](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1oh4FvXvWrliZ6UmRiF05fwadGEp7-Jkg-_e_82S7H6Q/edit?usp=sharing)

      **Best practices to fill it out:**

      - Avoid generic answers (‚Äúwe target tech companies‚Äù). Get really specific.
      - Use the client‚Äôs own language ‚Äî how do they describe their challenges?
    </Tip>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up their Tone of Voice together">
    The Tone of Voice is where you teach Scripe to sound like **you**: your phrasing, structure, energy, and personal style.

    Go through these steps together with your client:

    - **Enable Adaptive Tone of Voice** so Scripe to learn from existing LinkedIn posts and continuously refine your voice over time.
    - **Set post structure preferences** to define post length, sentence length, and emoji frequency.

    The following optional preferences can be done by the client later:

    - **Add a custom footer (optional)**
    - **Add inspiration (optional)**
    - **Fine-tune with advanced settings (optional)**
  </Step>
  <Step title="Create the Content Strategy together">
    Explain to the client that the [content strategy settings](https://scripe.mintlify.app/content-strategy/content-strategy) define what types of posts they want to publish, how often, and who they're trying to reach.

    It allows Scripe to recommend the best post formats for their goals and measure content performance in a way that actually makes sense to reach these goals.

    Follow the steps in the product to the set it up together.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once all of the above is done, Scripe will begin generating [weekly AI-powered Content Suggestions](https://scripe.mintlify.app/multiple-posts/ai-powered) (done-for-you post ideas). You‚Äôll revisit these suggestions together later in the call to review and refine them.

Now let's walk the client through the post generation, management and scheduling:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create the first scriped post together & teach them how to schedule it" stepNumber={6}>
    Start in the **Chat**: ask Scripe for 3 post ideas based on the client‚Äôs industry, then pick one to work on together.

    Once a draft is generated, move into the Scripe Editor:

    - Show the client they can directly edit the text.
    - Use the Editor Chat to keep refining the post until it feels right.

    From the Editor, explain the publishing options:

    - **Schedule post to LinkedIn** ‚Üí set the post for automatic publishing.
    - **Save as draft in the calendar** ‚Üí add the post to the calendar as a draft.
    - **Publish immediately** ‚Üí push it live on LinkedIn right away.

    Guide the client to schedule their first Scripe-generated post so the LinkedIn scheduling connection is set up, then show them the post in the Calendar view.

    <Info>
      Let the client know that the LinkedIn scheduling connection may occasionally expire. Scripe will flag this on the dashboard and prompt them to reconnect when needed.
    </Info>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Guide the client to connect Scripe with their WhatsApp" stepNumber={7}>
    Back to the dashboard, let your client know that they can also click on **Create Posts** on the top left corner of their dashboard to create posts in different ways.

    And one of the easiest ways is to install the [WhatsApp integration](/integrations/whats-app) to instantly send ideas or content snippets straight into Scripe whenever inspiration strikes.

    To install the WhatsApp integration, you can guide your client like this:

    1. In your dashboard sidebar, click on the WhatsApp symbol on the top right of the Chat.
    3. A QR code will be displayed.
    4. Open WhatsApp on your phone, scan the QR code, and you‚Äôre connected.
    5. Send an audio or a text message to see it in action.
    6. Go to Sources to see the input in there, click on it.
    7. Use the bar on the bottom and ask it to give you post ideas based on it.

    <Tip>
      They can use this bar to prompt or ask anything, for all types of sources.
    </Tip>
    ![Sources Bar](/images/sources-bar.jpg)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Guide the client through the Posts page" stepNumber={8}>
    The **Posts page** is where all content created in Scripe lives.

    Explain to the client that this page gives them a **clear overview and control** over everything they‚Äôve worked on: past, present, and upcoming.

    On this page, they can:

    - See all generated posts and drafts in one place
    - Drag and drop posts between statuses (e.g. idea, draft, review, scheduled, published)
    - Put a post under review and assign a reviewer
    - Open any post to edit, refine, or repurpose it
    - Schedule posts directly from this page

    Use this to show how easy it is to move content through a real workflow without losing track of anything.

    <Note>
      If your client has a specific workflow, for example, two review stages before a post goes live, you can set this up in the [Workspace Settings](/settings/post-status). Once configured, those custom statuses will appear on the Posts page and can be used via drag and drop like any other status.
    </Note>
  </Step>
  <Step title="Go through the AI-powered Content Suggestions" stepNumber={9}>
    The [AI-powered Content Suggestions](/post/ai-powered) might take a couple of hours to generate.

    Before ending the call, check in the dashboard, below the chat, if there are any suggestions ready to be shown to your client. Let them know that these suggestions are based on their content strategy, posting goals, and personal tone and are generated weekly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set Action Steps until next call or deadline." stepNumber={10}>
    We recommend that, as a next step, you ask the client to create and plan 2-3 posts and assign them to you for review.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you have time during the session, you can also introduce the Inspiration and Analytics pages to round out the session.
